Environmental Awareness

Environmental awareness is awareness for the environmental and everything around it, and the awareness that the Earth needs to be taken care of and preserved more.

The Importance of Environmental Awareness

It is important that people should be aware of the environment and planet Earth because we all need earth for a chance of survival as it is our only home. Damage to the environment could cause the extinction of an unreal amount of species, greatly reducing the biodiversity, and it could even lead to our own speciesâ€™ extinction.

Environmental Issues And Their Impact

Deforestation is one large issue involving the environment. Animals living within trees have their homes stripped away from them because of this action. They will be forced out in search of a new and safer place to call home. Trees also help turn carbon dioxide into the oxygen we need and breathe through photosynthesis, so not only is cutting down trees harmful for the environment, but also for our own species. Another issue involving the environment include but is not limited to the mass-production and littering of plastic. These plastics may end up in the water, where unsuspecting marine wildlife could consume and/or suffocate in the plastics thrown out, unbeknownst to themselves. The production of pollution originating from factories or vehicles is another issue. The smoke and pollution may reach the ozone layer and create a hole in it, allowing the Sun's ultraviolet rays to enter the atmosphere and onto the Earth, causing a plethora of problems. Health issues will arise both from the pollution and the ultraviolet rays.

Acid Rain

Lastly, acid rain is a large issue. Acid rain is caused by pollution emitting sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides into the air and coming in contact with rainwater, causing the pH of rainwater - usually around 5 - to drop to as acidic as 3. This might also pollute other bodies of water and the environment itself as everything will be filled with acid. Children who play out in the rain will be harmed by this acidic rainwater.

Impacts on The Environment

Effects of Environmental Degradation

Impacts on Human Health

Pollution in the air may cause respiratory diseases and issues. It may also cause skin cancer from the ultraviolet rays originating from the Sun passing through the ozone layer thinned by pollution. Leachate in the water caused by landfills may have hazardous effects on human health.

Impacts on Wildlife

The impact on the wildlife on Earth is similar to the impact on human health. Pollution may reach wildlife and will likely cause health issues for wildlife. Littering, notably plastic, will cause the deaths of different forms of wildlife. For example, sea turtles may be suffocated by plastic bags in which they unknowingly insert their heads into.

Overpopulation

Overpopulation may also become a problem. If there are too many humans and/or living organisms, there might not be enough resources to supply the demands of every single living organism on Earth. For example, too many humans may cause food supplies to run out, starting a global starvation.

Impact on Soil

Acid rain may affect the soil on the ground. The acidic conditions of the rainwater will damage the soil for future growth, which will impact the environment as living organisms such as plants won't be able to grow in soil impacted by acid rain.
